About

Shawn Lee is pursuing his master’s degree in Information Security at Carnegie Mellon University. He graduated first in his class when he received his Bachelor’s in Computer Science.

Shawn was a machine learning research assistant at Academia Sinica, the highest academy in Taiwan. He conducted research on the probability of users clicking on online advertisements while using less user information from other websites, which enhances users’ privacy. He was also a software-defined network testing intern at D-Link NCTU Joint Research Center. He collaborated with the engineering team to test the functionalities of a new switch and identified one critical difference between simulation software and hardware design that caused the switch to malfunction.

In addition to his professional experience, Shawn also has project experiences related to software engineering. He implemented concurrent caching web proxies on Amazon Web Service, performed targeted adversarial attacks against a keyword spotting system, and built the backend system of a sports day registration website. His solid academic and professional background makes him a good candidate for the company.
